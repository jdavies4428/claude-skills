## KEY INSIGHTS FROM RESEARCH

### Emotional Neuroscience
- Older adults experience **bittersweet nostalgia** (sadness + joy), which drives higher engagement than pure happiness
- **Eye contact** triggers immediate oxytocin/bonding response; eyes-first restoration (Concepts 4 & 8) is neurologically powerful
- **Music is the primary emotional delivery system** for 45+ demographic; audio on videos = 34.5% lower CPA than silent

### Platform Performance for 45+
- Facebook dominates (65+ users choose it over other platforms)
- **Reels 60-90 seconds** = optimal engagement window
- **Vertical 9:16 video WITH sound** significantly outperforms (34.5% lower CPA vs. silent)
- Older adults **watch longer videos than younger demos** and replay emotional moments
- **Share rate on emotional content** = 3-6% (vs. 0.5% average)

### Psychological Drivers
1. **Reunion with lost loved ones** (grief → hope arc)
2. **Legacy preservation** (existential purpose)
3. **Memory clarity** (fear of forgetting)
4. **Generational connection** (meaning-making)
5. **Time transcendence** (mortality anxiety)

### What Moves Them to Tears (Then Action)
- Direct eye contact in restored photo
- Handwritten context (names, dates, relationships)
- "Before it's too late" messaging paired with hope
- Slow pacing (2-3 second beats, not snappy cuts)
- Warm color grading (sepia, golden tones)
- Music that crescendos at clarity moment
- Tactile elements (album, leather, paper texture)

## PRODUCTION SPECIFICATIONS

### Format
- **Duration:** 60-90 seconds (75s optimal)
- **Aspect Ratio:** 9:16 vertical (mobile-first)
- **First 3 Seconds:** Must hook with problem + emotional context (CRITICAL)
- **Pacing:** Minimum 2-3 second beats between transitions
- **Color Grading:** Warm throughout (sepia, golden, amber tones)
- **Text:** Handwritten cursive style, sepia-toned, readable

### Audio (CRITICAL)
- Always include sound (music + subtle ambient)
- **Music Arc:** Quiet opening → building mid-section → emotional peak → gentle sustain
- **Recommended styles:** Piano, music box, strings, cello, soft acoustic guitar
- **Avoid:** Trendy sounds, EDM, trap beats, silence, modern effects

### Animation Principles
- Particles move slowly and meaningfully (not aggressive)
- Ken Burns effect (gentle zoom/pan) for detail discovery
- No glitch effects or trendy transitions
- Soft light overlays (window sunlight aesthetic)
- Upward floating particles = transcendence; downward = settling/peace

## COPY STRATEGY

### Hook Formula (First 3 Seconds)
**[Emotional vulnerability] + [Problem context] + [Implied promise]**

Examples:
- "You're afraid you're forgetting. Let us bring them back."
- "Before it's lost forever. Restore your family's story now."
- "The people you love deserve to be seen clearly."

### CTA Formula (Legacy Language > Urgency)
**Avoid:** "Restore now! Limited time offer!"
**Use:** "Preserve your family's legacy," "Unlock your family's story," "Protect the memories that matter"

### Best Performing Copy
"The photos you're afraid of losing. Restored, preserved, passed on."
(Combines urgency + agency + legacy across full emotional journey)

## CREATIVE GUIDELINES (DO/DON'T)

### DO THIS
✅ Slow, contemplative pacing (2-3 second beats)
✅ Warm color grading (sepia, golden, amber)
✅ Handwritten text elements (personal, intimate)
✅ Music that builds to emotional peak
✅ Soft particle effects (meaningful, not aggressive)
✅ Family-focused language
✅ Respect for loss while offering hope
✅ Eye contact moments (proven trigger)
✅ Textured, tactile visuals
✅ 2-3 second "hold" moments for emotion processing

### DON'T DO THIS
❌ Fast cuts or transitions
❌ Modern sound effects (whooshes, beeps)
❌ Trendy music or TikTok sounds
❌ Bright, oversaturated, overly modern colors
❌ Sudden particle explosions
❌ Tech language ("AI-powered," "machine learning")
❌ Silent videos (audio critical for impact)
❌ Upbeat, energetic pacing
❌ Irony or humor about loss/memory
❌ Glitch effects
